                                                          Ladan Osman


                                                          

I follow



                                                          

the mirage of a man



                             
and his son in a boat.

                                                 

They drift

on the shifting
dune peaks,
they raise

their shoulders

against the wind.

I call to them, my voice a large dog in a crowded yard.


                                                                                       
Do they also holler at the sun?
I have no faculty to hear them.

           On             Earth             I             made             men             into             mist            , and             now             feel             my             own             dust             wander,             lift,             and             swirl.


                                   In the Afterlife,

the weight of bodies is heavy on the scale.



                           If I were allowed to cry,
                                       my tears would rust its beams.

In the Afterlife,








                        their weight is a smoking fuse.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                       Their souls dont extinguish, they

ignite

and

reignite and never explode.



                                                                                                                                                                                   I wait.
                                                   They tamp bodies in the Afterlife.
                           They show me the razors edge, tell me to walk along it without looking down.
                            They make figures that stay at a distance.                                                

    I confront their backs.
